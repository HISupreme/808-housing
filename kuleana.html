Kuleana tax exemption

If your family owns kuleana land — land your kūpuna received under the Kuleana Act of 1850 — you may qualify to pay $0–$300 a year in property tax instead of thousands. This walkthrough helps you find out and file.
Built for Native Hawaiian families as a free, private tool. No tracking, no signup, no data collected. Information sourced from the Office of Hawaiian Affairs, Native Hawaiian Legal Corporation, and the four county real property tax offices. The kuleana exemption is governed by HRS 246-31 and county ordinances. This tool is for orientation, not legal advice — for help with quiet title disputes, partition actions, or contested ancestry, contact Native Hawaiian Legal Corporation directly. Filing deadline in all counties is September 30 for the following tax year.
